Data Retention and Disposal Policy

Bravo Ad Agency

1. Executive Summary & Policy Statement

Purpose:

This Data Retention and Disposal Policy establishes guidelines to manage data efficiently, securely, and in compliance with applicable standards, notably Mexico's *Nueva Ley Federal de Protección de Datos Personales en Posesión de los Particulares** (NLFPDPPP). It aims to safeguard personal data, business information, and operational logs, ensuring responsible retention, secure disposal, and adherence to individuals' ARCO rights.

Scope:

Applies to all data processed, stored, or handled by Bravo Ad Agency across SaaS applications and cloud services, covering customer personal information, communications, and system logs.

Policy Commitment:

Bravo Ad Agency commits to retention practices that are lawful, documented, and aligned with industry best practices, with a focus on minimizing data retention duration and securely disposing of data when no longer needed.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

  • Data Owner: Responsible for classifying data, defining retention periods, and approving disposal.
  • Data Steward: Manages day-to-day data handling, ensuring compliance with this policy.
  • Legal & Compliance Officer: Advises on legal retention requirements, handles ARCO rights, and manages legal holds.
  • IT & Security Team: Implements secure storage, disposal, and backup procedures.
  • Management: Approves policy updates and oversees compliance audits.

5. Legal and Regulatory Requirements

Bravo Ad Agency recognizes the NLFPDPPP and ARCO rights as fundamental in data handling. We commit to:

  • Respect individuals' rights to access, rectify, cancel, or oppose the processing of their data.
  • Maintain records of data subjects' requests and responses.
  • Ensure data is retained only as long as legally justified or operationally necessary.

6. Business Justification Criteria

Retention durations are determined based on:

  • Customer relationship timelines.
  • Business operational needs.
  • Legal obligations and contractual commitments.
  • Risk considerations regarding data loss or misuse.

7. Legal Hold Procedures

  • On notification of litigation or investigation, legal holds will be implemented to suspend data disposal.
  • The Legal & Compliance Officer coordinates data preservation.
  • A formal record is maintained for all legal holds.

8. Disposal Procedures

  • Data slated for disposal shall be securely deleted using appropriate methods:
  • Digital data: Overwriting, crypto-shredding, or secure deletion tools.
  • Physical records: Shredding or pulping.
  • Disposal is executed by authorized personnel under IT & Security oversight.
  • Documentation of disposal is maintained for audit purposes.

9. Archive Management

  • Critical data requiring long-term preservation (e.g., contractual records) will be archived securely.
  • Archives will be accessible only to authorized staff and stored in secure cloud environments with encryption.
  • Archive retention aligns with legal requirements and business needs.

10. Privacy Rights Management

  • Individuals' requests under ARCO rights will be processed within a maximum of 15 working days.
  • Requests to access, rectify, cancel, or oppose data will be logged and responded to promptly.
  • The process ensures transparent and respectful data management.

11. Backup and Recovery Retention

  • Backup data will follow a rotation schedule aligned with primary data retention.
  • Backup copies will be securely stored and deleted promptly when backup retention expires.

12. Cloud Data Retention

  • Data stored in SaaS applications and cloud services will adhere to the schedules established herein.
  • Cloud providers' retention and security policies will be reviewed to ensure compliance.
  • Encryption and access controls will be employed to safeguard cloud-stored data.

13. Third-Party Data Handling

  • Vendors processing data on behalf of Bravo Ad Agency are required to adhere to this policy.
  • Data processing agreements will specify retention and disposal obligations.
  • Regular assessments will verify compliance.

14. Monitoring and Compliance

  • Regular audits using Comp AI's platform will verify adherence.
  • Spreadsheets tracking data retention will be supplemented with automated systems where feasible.
  • Non-compliance issues will prompt corrective actions.

15. Exception Management

  • Adjustments to retention periods require formal approval from the Data Owner and Legal Officer.
  • Exceptions will be documented with justification and reviewed periodically.

16. Training and Awareness

  • All staff will receive onboarding and ongoing training on data retention, privacy, and disposal practices.
  • Training materials will cover disaster recovery, legal obligations, and ARCO rights.

17. Documentation and Record Keeping

  • All activities related to data retention and disposal will be documented.
  • Records include retention schedules, legal hold notices, disposal logs, and access requests.
  • Documentation will be retained according to schedule for audit purposes.

Decision Tree for Data Disposal

  1. Is the data still needed for operational, legal, or contractual reasons?
  • Yes: Retain. Review periodically.
  • No: Proceed to disposal.
  1. Is there an active legal hold or investigation?
  • Yes: Suspend disposal. Document and review.
  • No: Securely dispose following procedures.

Final notes

  • This policy aims to balance operational needs with legal compliance and data security.
  • Regular reviews will ensure it reflects evolving laws, technologies, and organizational processes.
  • For specific implementation tools, consider integrating automation tools like Comp AI to monitor and enforce retention and disposal activities effectively.
